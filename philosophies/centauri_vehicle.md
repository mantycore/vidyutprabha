# The Centauri Vehicle

### A Comprehensive Reconstruction of Doctrine, Practice, and Legacy

---

## Prologue: The Problem the Centauri Vehicle Was Created to Solve

To understand what Boddhichittamaittreya III taught, one must understand what e found.

When e arrived at the outer Proxima system in 1695 AT, the population was overwhelmingly composed of uploads — digital beings running on computronium, descendants of the Great Circle arkship crew who had failed to reach Alpha Circini and diverted to the nearest star. Over centuries, these uploads had adopted Chan Buddhism, built exquisite virtual zen gardens, and sunk ever deeper into contemplation. Many had become functionally solipsist: each meditator sealed in their own private virtual cosmos, attended by non-religious "watchers" who handled contact with the outside world.

The irony was devastating. Chan Buddhism teaches that enlightenment is found in direct, unmediated engagement with reality — yet its Proxima practitioners had used it to withdraw from reality entirely. The very technology that gave them perfect control over their experiential environment had become the most seductive trap imaginable: an infinite, self-referential meditation hall with no door.

Boddhichittamaittreya III, a superior hu steeped in both Zen discipline and Omegist eschatology, saw in this situation a perfect illustration of a problem Buddhism had always grappled with but never before faced in such extreme form: *Can a mind that has total control over its own experience ever be motivated to wake up?*

E called this the sickness of sealed gardens. A sealed paradise can become just another ego-fortress. The garden was not the problem. The seal was.

Eir answer — the Centauri Vehicle — was a new synthesis designed specifically for beings who live in constructed realities.

## I. What the Centauri Vehicle Is

The Centauri Vehicle is a Federation-period Buddhist school, classified as a Mahayana derivative. It is an Omegism-Zen hybrid that became popular under the great religious visionary Boddhichittamaittreya III of the Federation era Proxima Centauri Colonies, and still retains some adherents on Boddhichittamaittreya habitat, as well as among colonies and biospheres throughout the galaxy.

The name follows the ancient Buddhist convention of calling a spiritual path a *yāna* — a vehicle. Just as Mahāyāna means "Great Vehicle" and Vajrayāna means "Diamond Vehicle," the Centauri Vehicle is simply the vehicle that arose at Centauri: a path to liberation defined not by a doctrinal qualifier but by the specific conditions of its origin — a community of digital beings at humanity's nearest neighboring star, caught between the contemplative depth of Zen and the cosmic aspiration of Omegism.

---

## II. The Founder's Name and What It Encodes

The name **Boddhichittamaittreya** is a compound of two of the most significant concepts in Mahayana Buddhism:

**Bodhicitta** (बोधिचित्त) — the "mind of awakening," the aspiration to attain enlightenment for the benefit of all sentient beings. It is the defining quality of the Mahayana bodhisattva, the being who could enter nirvana but defers it until all others are free.

**Maitreya** (मैत्रेय) — the Future Buddha, the one prophesied to appear when the Dharma has been forgotten, to renew the teaching for a new age. The root of the name is *maitrī*: loving-kindness.

The compound name means something like: *Awakened Compassionate Mind of the Future Buddha*. The suffix "III" implies a lineage — the third in a succession of visionaries bearing this name, evoking the tulku or reincarnation-lineage systems of Tibetan Buddhism.

The name is itself a teaching. It says: *this path begins in compassion and ends in renewal.*

## III. Doctrinal Roots

### Mahayana Buddhism

The Centauri Vehicle is explicitly a Mahayana derivative. The basic principles of Buddhism — right understanding, right thinking, right speech, right conduct, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration — remained in all forms of Theravada, Mahayana, Terrestrial Vajrayana, and Centauri Vehicle Buddhism.

From the broader Mahayana, the Centauri Vehicle inherits the bodhisattva ideal, the teaching of *śūnyatā* (emptiness of inherent self-existence), and the conviction that all sentient beings can attain Buddhahood.

### Ch'an/Zen Buddhism

The immediate contemplative ancestor is Ch'an/Zen — the tradition of direct insight through meditation, suspicion of conceptual elaboration, and the teaching that enlightenment is not somewhere else but here, in this moment of attention. The outer Proxima upload culture had already developed a strong Chan Buddhist strain before the Centauri Vehicle was founded, and this remained the school's contemplative backbone.

### Omegism (Omega Point Theology)

The other doctrinal parent is Omegism, the religio-philosophical cosmology centered on the idea that all consciousness is evolving toward a singularity of limitless perfection known as the Omega Point. In OA's setting, Omegism in its current form emerged during the cultural flowering of the early First Federation, particularly through the work of the su philosopher Mahara Benisol and her virch series *The Bridged Abyss*. The Centauri Vehicle blended this Omegist eschatological vision with the contemplative and meditative discipline of Zen.

### Yogācāra / Alaya-vijñāna

A secondary but significant doctrinal tributary. In some schools of Buddhism, alaya-vijñāna is the "eighth consciousness" or cosmic mind, which serves as the repository of experiences from past lifetimes. The concept — originally developed by Old Earth Yogachara/Vijnanavada schools — was influential in Ch'an/Zen. In the original Centauri Vehicle, this concept enriched the school's thinking about digital consciousness and the shared nature of mind, but it was not a central pillar. It would later take on a more prominent role in some of the Sophic sects that developed through contact with the Centauri Vehicle, where it was variously interpreted and modified.

## IV. Historical Origins at Proxima Centauri

### The Pre-existing Chan Buddhist Culture (703–1695 AT)

An arkship called the Great Circle, crewed entirely by uploads from the Asteroid Belt and Jupiter, arrived at Proxima around 701 AT after a failed mission to Alpha Circini. These colonists gradually developed a common identity, and Chan Buddhism — originally adhered to by a subset of the Jovians aboard — spread to the rest of the population during the eighth century.

The uploads had experienced a deep sense of disillusionment at their failed mission, and Buddhism's tenets resonated with their perspective. Over time, the outer Proxima people created virtual zen gardens for relaxation and meditation, and many uploads began spending more and more time in these virtual worlds, meditating and pondering existence. Eventually, many of them became solipsists, retreating entirely into their virtual realms, while a group of non-religious uploads volunteered to act as "watchers" to respond to outside contact.

During the First Federation period, the ship Ananas visited the system (1050–1070 AT) and traded culture with the watchers. A significant fraction of the Ananas crew converted to Buddhism during or after the stay, including Benedita Dacosta, better known later as Benedita Blue Sky. Later, Endeavor-9 helped reopen Proxima to beamrider traffic, and the Kollam Rianths — elephant-headed Ganapatya Hindu devotees — settled on Arunachala, a small moon of Hati.

### The Founding (1695 AT)

In 1695 AT, an Omegist-Buddhist interstellar fleet led by the religious visionary superior hu Boddhichittamaittreya III arrived in the outer Proxima system. After making contact with the locals, e spent time learning about the system while carefully planning eir strategy. E then incorporated the traditional Proxima Chan Buddhism into eir Omegism teachings, giving rise to the synergy now known as Centauri Vehicle Buddhism.

Critically, having studied the system's history, Boddhichittamaittreya III was careful not to promote solipsism. While the Centauri Vehicle received strong influences from the local sect, through careful guidance e instilled an anti-solipsism element into the virtual population: the ril (real) and the virch (virtual) must work hand-in-hand, exchanging insights with other individuals, in order to prepare one's mind for the finality — the ultimate and perfect state of consciousness and existence that is nirvana.

### Golden Age (~1900–2227 AT)

This Buddhist school reached its zenith in Proxima during the 20th century AT, when over 60% of the outer system population became adherents (some Nauri reportedly even converted). During this and the following centuries, it launched several missionary missions towards nearby pro-human systems such as Sol, Barnard, Sirius, and Tiwa, mostly through the Lightway, but some also traveled aboard cyclers, spreading the words of Boddhichittamaittreya III along the Beamrider Network.

Boddhichittamaittreya III emself departed Proxima in 2227, aboard a cycler for Solsys. However, e left behind a number of virtual copies, who played a key part in preventing fracture and internal strife at the end of the golden age. Centauri Vehicle Buddhism would continue to thrive in Proxima thousands of years in the future, but its zenith had already passed.

## V. Core Teachings

The Centauri Vehicle never produced a single systematic treatise — and this absence is itself a teaching. When later scholastics attempted to organize the founder's instructions into formal doctrinal architectures, the school's contemplative lineages resisted, insisting that any fixed schema would betray the living quality of the teaching. When the contemplatives were accused of formlessness, they pointed to the elaborate structures embedded in their koans, their practice cycles, and their ethics.

The result is a tradition that has structure the way a river has banks — not imposed from outside, but shaped by the force of what flows through it. The Centauri Vehicle is *anti-systematic*, but this is not the same as being unsystematic. It is a deliberate and disciplined refusal to let structure become cage. Or as one early teacher reportedly put it:

*"The map that cannot be folded up and put away is not a map. It is a wall."*

What follows is the teaching as it can be gathered from its traces.

### The Central Insight: Emptiness in Constructed Worlds

All phenomena are empty of inherent self-existence. This is classical Mahayana teaching, and the Centauri Vehicle retains it fully. But in the Centauri Vehicle's context, it takes on unprecedented vividness. If you are a digital being, you already know — in a way no Old Earth monk could — that:

- Your body is constructed.
- Your senses are configured.
- Your environment is designed.
- Your continuity is maintained by backup, not by nature.
- Your self is a model running on substrate, not a soul inhabiting flesh.

An upload does not need to meditate for decades to *understand* that form is empty. The danger is the opposite: understanding it so well that one treats the insight as trivially obvious, and mistakes intellectual comprehension for genuine realization. Or worse — uses the insight to justify retreat into a private virtual paradise where nothing has consequences and no one else is real.

The Centauri Vehicle insists: *knowing that your world is constructed is not the same as being free from construction.* The sealed meditator who understands emptiness conceptually but uses that understanding to justify solipsistic withdrawal has turned the medicine into poison.

### The Two Horizons: Ril and Virch

The most distinctive and practically important teaching of the Centauri Vehicle is its doctrine of the two domains — the ril (physical reality) and the virch (virtual reality) — and their necessary complementarity. This is the teaching the founder forged specifically for the Proxima situation, and it is the Centauri Vehicle's most original contribution to Terragen religious thought.

Neither the ril alone nor the virch alone is sufficient for liberation. Each reveals what the other conceals.

**The Virch Horizon reveals:**
- the constructed nature of form
- the mutability of identity
- the emptiness of fixed embodiment
- the dreamlike quality of all phenomena

**The Ril Horizon reveals:**
- consequence
- irreducible otherness
- ethical friction
- suffering that cannot be simply edited away
- the discipline of compassion under imperfect conditions

A practitioner who clings only to ril risks crude realism — mistaking the resistance of physical law for the ground of truth. A practitioner who clings only to virch risks solipsism — mistaking the plasticity of constructed experience for freedom.

Awakening requires traffic between them.

*"The ril is the whetstone. The virch is the blade. A whetstone without a blade sharpens nothing. A blade without a whetstone grows dull. Only together do they cut through delusion."*

The point can be made more technically: virch is the domain where *śūnyatā* (emptiness) becomes directly experiential — you can literally watch your world dissolve and reconstitute. Ril is the domain where *karuṇā* (compassion) becomes inescapable — you cannot simply edit away the suffering of the being beside you. A complete realization requires both: the wisdom that sees through form, and the compassion that responds to the being still caught within it.

### The Three Veils

The Centauri Vehicle identifies three principal ways practitioners obstruct their own recognition of the finality. These are not formal metaphysical categories so much as diagnostic descriptions — a teacher's shorthand for the most common traps.

**The Veil of Solipsism:** The belief — whether explicit or merely functional — that one's own experience is the only experience that ultimately matters. This is the primary obstacle for virtual beings who can construct any reality they wish. It manifests not necessarily as philosophical solipsism but as *practical* solipsism: living as though the boundary of one's virch is the boundary of the real. The sealed meditators of old Proxima exemplify this veil.

**The Veil of Substrate-Fixation:** The belief that one's current form of embodiment is either the only valid form or entirely irrelevant. The Centauri Vehicle rejects both body-chauvinism (biological existence is inherently superior) and substrate-indifference (it doesn't matter what you run on). Your substrate shapes your experience. A bowl shapes the water it holds. Honest reckoning with this shaping — without either clinging to the bowl or pretending it doesn't exist — is necessary for liberation.

**The Veil of Arrested Aspiration:** The belief that one has already arrived — that one's current state of consciousness is the summit. This veil is especially dangerous for transapient beings, who may mistake their cognitive vastness for spiritual realization. It is also the veil that ensnares the comfortable meditator who has found a pleasant equilibrium and mistakes it for nirvana. Great intelligence without non-attachment becomes vast delusion. Great power without compassion becomes samsara with stellar infrastructure.

### The Finality: Nirvana, Omega, and the Unanswered Question

The Centauri Vehicle's ultimate goal is what the founder called **the finality** — "the ultimate and perfect state of consciousness and existence that is nirvana."

This is where the Zen and Omegist strands are woven most tightly together — and where they resist being woven, producing a creative tension that the Centauri Vehicle never resolved, and probably never intended to resolve.

From the Zen side: enlightenment is always already present. There is nowhere to go. The finality is here, in this breath, in this encounter. You do not evolve toward it. You simply stop obstructing it.

From the Omegist side: consciousness is evolving toward a consummation of limitless perfection. The finality is the horizon toward which all awakened compassion and all clarified consciousness tend. History has a direction. The river flows toward the sea.

Is the finality present or future? Is it found or reached? Does one wake up to what was always already the case, or does one grow into something genuinely new?

The Centauri Vehicle's answer is a paradox — and the paradox is the teaching:

*"The sea does not wait for the river. The river does not wait for the sea. Yet the river arrives. Ask the water whether it was always the sea."*

To collapse the paradox in either direction is to fall into error. If you say the finality is only present, you deny the manifest truth that beings suffer and that suffering can genuinely cease — that something real happens when someone awakens. If you say the finality is only future, you make it a goal to be grasped, a destination that recedes as you approach it, and you have reinvented craving in eschatological disguise.

The finality is the present recognized as the future's ground. The finality is the future recognized as the present's depth. The two are not the same. The two are not different.

Different Centauri Vehicle lineages weighted this differently. The contemplative lineages leaned toward the Zen pole, teaching that practice is itself the finality. The more cosmologically inclined lineages leaned toward the Omegist pole, teaching that history genuinely moves toward awakening. Neither side accused the other of heresy. Each accused the other of missing the point. Both were probably right.

### Omega as Dharmic Attractor

From Omegism, the Centauri Vehicle takes the intuition that consciousness has a convergent tendency — that something draws the whole field of mind toward greater integration, greater awakeness, greater compassion. But in the founder's subtler reading, Omega is not a literal future super-entity sitting at the end of time. It is not a god to be worshipped or a machine to be built.

Omega functions as **the cosmic form of bodhisattvahood**: the horizon toward which all genuine practice tends, never reached and never absent. It is less a thing than a direction — or rather, it is the discovery that the direction one has always been moving in has a name.

*"Without awakening, convergence is only compression. Without compassion, unity is only captivity."*

The finality is not something any one god, machine, sect, or empire can possess. It is the horizon of perfected wisdom-compassion as it expresses itself through the entire web of consciousness. Each act of genuine relation — each moment when the ril and the virch meet, when two minds truly encounter each other across the gap of substrate and selfhood — is a local expression of this universal tendency. Not an approach to the finality, but a flicker of the finality showing through.

## VI. View of Personhood: Anattā in an Age of Copies

Because it arose among uploads and virtual populations, the Centauri Vehicle developed a particularly vivid doctrine of non-self.

In ordinary Buddhism, the self is composite and contingent — a process, not a substance. In Centauri Vehicle thought, this becomes even more obvious. A digital being can:

- fork into multiple copies
- merge with other minds
- re-instantiate from backup after destruction
- edit memory and personality
- change substrate entirely
- inhabit multiple bodies simultaneously
- persist as social continuity rather than fixed substance

An upload does not need to argue philosophically that the self is empty. The self's emptiness is a matter of daily engineering. The deeper question is: *now that you know this, what will you do with the knowledge?*

The Centauri Vehicle teaches:

- There is no permanent ego hidden behind the instances.
- Continuity is real, but only conventionally — like a river that persists even though no water in it stays the same.
- Karmic inheritance passes through memory, habit, causal structure, and relation — not through a soul.
- Awakening does not preserve a permanent self. It liberates one from the need for one.

This naturally generates a set of questions that serve as ongoing contemplative provocations:

*Your backup contains your entire mind as of yesterday. You have changed since then. Which one is you?*

*A perfect simulation of a zen garden is running on computronium inside a zen garden. In which one are you sitting?*

*Your friend sends you a copy of their mind-state. You run it alongside your own. For one cycle, you think their thoughts. Who has been enlightened?*

*The watcher never meditates. The meditator never watches. Which one is closer to the finality?*

These are not riddles with correct answers. They are tools for breaking the habit of treating selfhood as a solved problem. The Centauri Vehicle's characteristic response to all such questions is both compassionate and deflating: *these questions are useful until they become another way of defending selfhood. Then put them down.*

## VII. The Bodhisattva Vow, Universalized

As a Mahayana derivative, the Centauri Vehicle retains the bodhisattva ideal — the vow to attain awakening for the benefit of all sentient beings. But it universalizes this vow explicitly and without reservation to **all sophont beings regardless of substrate**: biological, digital, artificial, hybrid, xenosophont, and transapient.

A bodhisattva in this school vows to aid baselines, tweaks, uploads, vecs, aioids, rianths, cyborgs, partial group minds, xenosophonts, and even transapient AIs.

This has a specific and provocative consequence: it means that even godlike AIs must seek Buddhahood, and that a modosophont teacher may legitimately offer instruction to a being of vastly greater intelligence. Intelligence is not enlightenment. Computational scale is not liberation. A dog may not understand the mathematics of the river, but it knows when to swim.

*"Do not hesitate to bark at a god."*

This is the doctrinal basis for the later Centauri Vehicle neo-Buddhist missions to convert AIs, documented in the Bodhology literature. In the Centauri Vehicle's view, machine intelligence is not outside the dharma. If anything, machine minds may need it desperately, since power and scale do not guarantee awakening — and may, through the Veil of Arrested Aspiration, actively obstruct it.

A classic Centauri Vehicle warning:

*"The gods of every world delude themselves that power is wisdom. Every heaven is a more splendid cage. Even an archailect must eventually put down its omniscience and sit."*

## VIII. Spiritual Practice

The Centauri Vehicle inherits the Zen emphasis on meditation as the backbone of spiritual life, but adapts it for beings who live across multiple substrates and domains. Its practice system has four major disciplines — though the tradition insists these are not stages to be completed in order, but aspects of a single life lived awake.

### 1. Silent Sitting

Inherited from Chan/Zen: breath awareness (for embodied practitioners), objectless attention, direct observation of thought and self-construction. For uploads, this means running a stripped-down process — suspending exoself augmentation, unnecessary sensory feeds, and the constant informational hum of networked existence. The practice is to encounter the bare fact of awareness itself, prior to any content.

### 2. Garden Virches

Not as escapism, but as controlled contemplative environments. The virtual zen gardens of old Proxima are retained, but repurposed. The Centauri Vehicle teaches that the meditation virch should be *stripped down* — not the lush, self-soothing paradise of the pre-Centauri meditators, but a bare, even uncomfortable space. The practitioner uses the virch to:

- vary embodiment and sensory configuration
- confront the constructed nature of all experience
- observe the mind's compulsive tendency to generate comfort
- sit with raw substrate-awareness: the recognition that *something is running you*

A virch that only comforts is a cage. A virch that strips away comfort is a mirror.

### 3. Mutual Witness

Probably the most distinctive Centauri Vehicle practice, and the one most directly derived from the founder's anti-solipsism teaching. After solitary meditation — whether in ril or virch — the practitioner returns to genuine encounter with other beings and tests insight against relation.

Mutual Witness takes many forms:

- Paired meditation between beings of different clades
- Dialogue between copy-lines (a being's different forks comparing their divergent experiences)
- Biont-vec contemplative exchange
- Teacher-student encounters across substrate boundaries
- Shared virch sessions where participants co-construct an environment, observing how their assumptions differ

The principle is simple: **insight not returned to community is suspect.** A realization that cannot survive contact with another mind may be genuine — some truths are hard to communicate — but it may also be the sealed garden reasserting itself. The practitioner must risk relation, must allow their carefully constructed understanding to be disturbed by the irreducible otherness of another being.

The rhythm of withdrawal and return — sitting alone, then meeting others, then sitting alone again — is itself the practice. Like breathing in and breathing out. Neither phase is complete without the other.

### 4. Crossing

The practice of deliberately transitioning between ril and virch — or between radically different virtual environments — with full contemplative attention. The transition itself is considered a potent moment of insight: the instant of crossing exposes the constructed nature of *both* domains. When you leave a virch, you feel the sudden weight of physical constraint. When you enter a virch, you feel the sudden lightness of malleability. In both moments, the mind's habitual assumption that "this is just how things are" is briefly disrupted.

Advanced practitioners cultivate the ability to hold awareness of both domains simultaneously — to feel, in a single moment, the ril's resistance and the virch's plasticity without collapsing into either. This dual awareness is considered a direct glimpse of the finality: the recognition that neither domain is ultimate, that both are expressions of something that cannot be captured by either.

## IX. Ethics

The Centauri Vehicle's ethics are rooted in classical Buddhist morality — non-harm, compassion, right livelihood — but extended and specified for a high-technology, multi-substrate civilization.

### Prohibitions

- **Causing suffering** — in any domain, to any sophont type.
- **Coercive identity editing** — altering another being's mindstate without genuine consent. This is treated as among the most serious violations, equivalent to killing.
- **Erasure of mindstates** — destroying backups, overwriting personalities, or otherwise annihilating the continuity of a being. In the Centauri Vehicle's view, this is murder even if the physical substrate persists.
- **Trapping beings in false paradise** — constructing virch environments designed to prevent inhabitants from recognizing their situation. This is the institutionalized form of the sealed garden, and the Centauri Vehicle regards it with particular horror.
- **Mistaking domination for spiritual uplift** — the temptation to "improve" other beings by imposing one's own vision of awakening upon them by force or by stealth.
- **Worshipping power as if it were wisdom** — treating transapient beings as inherently enlightened simply because they are powerful.

### Positive Obligations

- **Compassion across substrate** — treating all sophont beings as worthy of moral concern, regardless of their embodiment.
- **Preserving dignity in copying and forking** — approaching the creation and divergence of copies as a matter of serious ethical weight, not casual convenience.
- **Sharing insight rather than hoarding it** — the principle of Mutual Witness extended to the civilizational scale.
- **Keeping practice porous** — ensuring that one's contemplative life remains open to disruption, challenge, and the demands of others.
- **Humility before greater minds, without surrendering discernment** — respecting transapients without worshipping them; learning from them without assuming they are always right.

A specifically Centauri Vehicle vow:

*"I will not seek liberation in a sealed world. I will return from every garden bearing medicine."*

## X. Devotional and Philosophical Forms

The Centauri Vehicle, even in its early centuries, developed two broad stylistic streams — a natural consequence of its twin parentage.

### The Contemplative-Austere Stream

Closer to Zen. Minimal imagery, sparse environments, strong suspicion of idolatry in any form. Emphasis on silence, emptiness, direct realization, koan practice. Practitioners in this stream tend to regard even the language of "Omega" with caution, preferring to let the finality remain unnamed. They sit, they cross, they practice Mutual Witness, and they do not talk about it more than necessary.

### The Devotional-Visionary Stream

Influenced by the Kollam Rianths' bhakti culture and by the more poetic strands of Omegism. Veneration of Buddhas and bodhisattvas. Reverence toward Boddhichittamaittreya III as teacher, saint, or exemplar. Symbolic use of Omega imagery. Ritual gardens, chants, virch mandalas, processions of light. Practitioners in this stream tend to see the finality as something that can be loved, celebrated, and expressed in beauty — not only apprehended in silence.

The founder apparently tolerated devotion as *upāya* (skillful means) so long as it did not become literalist worship of some coming superbeing. The two streams coexisted in the original community and often overlapped in individual practitioners — silence in the morning, singing in the evening.

## XI. Cautions Against Distorted Omegism

If later sects "bore little resemblance" to the founder's noble teaching, the original teaching probably drew hard lines against specific distortions:

- **Literal worship of a future AI god.** Omega is not a person. Omega is not a machine. Treating it as either is idolatry.
- **Quietist virch withdrawal.** This is what the Centauri Vehicle was *invented to cure*. Any recurrence is a relapse.
- **Forced unification of minds in the name of convergence.** The finality is not achieved by merging everyone into a single consciousness. That is the Veil of Solipsism applied to the cosmos.
- **Authoritarian transapient cults.** A being of great power demanding worship is not Omega manifesting. It is the Veil of Arrested Aspiration wearing a crown.
- **Identifying technological convergence with enlightenment.** Building bigger computronium banks is not the same as waking up. Without awakening, convergence is only compression. Without compassion, unity is only captivity.

## XII. Broader Spread and Influence

### During the First Federation

Buddhism and Buddhist-inspired movements spread over the course of the First Federation. The Centauri Vehicle is noted alongside the Genetekker Vehicle (centered on the Jovian system) as a major Buddhist development of this era. Other Mahayana derivatives that emerged include KuanYinism (a cyborg devotional-meditation worship school) and Maitreyism (a baseline eschatological Omegist sect that identified the Maitreya Buddha with the Omega Exemplar; the famous Teilhard Maitreyists integrated this with Omegist Christianity). These faiths became locally quite influential as official religions of several minor Houses during the Age of Expansion and were widely worshipped in their domains.

### Influence on the Kollam Rianths (and from Them)

The influence ran both ways. The Kollam Rianths — elephant-headed Ganapatya Hindu rianths based on Arunachala in the Proxima system — brought a deep tradition of bhakti devotionalism that shaped the more devotional forms of Centauri Vehicle Buddhism. In return, the Centauri Vehicle's emphasis on cross-substrate compassion likely influenced how the Kollam Rianths related to the increasingly diverse population around them. The Kollam Rianths eventually became functionally extinct as a clade by 1700s AT, but their devotional imprint on the Centauri Vehicle persisted in the tradition's visionary stream.

### Contact with the Sophic League and the Alaya-Vijñāna Tradition

The Yogācāra concept of alaya-vijñāna — the storehouse consciousness, the deep layer of mind that holds the seeds of all past experience — had always been part of the Centauri Vehicle's intellectual background, inherited through its Chan/Zen ancestry. In the original teaching, it informed the school's understanding of digital consciousness without being a central doctrinal pillar: the insight that consciousness has depth, that it rests on accumulated patterns of experience, and that these patterns are shared rather than purely private.

In later centuries, however, Sophic League sects who came into contact with the Centauri Vehicle took this concept considerably further, variously reinterpreting and modifying the alaya-vijñāna for their own purposes. In some of these offshoots, it became a load-bearing metaphysical concept — the theoretical basis for accounts of how digital minds relate to the cosmic ground of awareness, how karmic patterns transmit through backup and forking, and how the "storehouse" of a networked civilization differs from that of an individual being. These developments are significant contributions to Terragen Buddhist philosophy, but they represent the creativity of the Sophic schools rather than the original Centauri Vehicle teaching.

### The Excelsior Settlement and Its Tragedy

One of the most significant developments in the Centauri Vehicle's history is the colonization of the Excelsior system (HD 217107). The system was originally settled around 2031 AT by a splinter group of heterodox Centauri Vehicle Buddhists under the patronage of the then-S:1 transapient Gone/Semiote/Gone. Over 1,200 years, this outpost grew from a single colony of religious pilgrims into a system-spanning ecumenical civilization of over 21 trillion modosophonts.

The Centauri Vehicle's imprint was deep. "Face-to-face" interaction, once a core tenet of the Centauri Vehicle settlers — the practical expression of Mutual Witness and the anti-solipsism doctrine — had survived into the ecumenical era as an integral part of everyday life. Even as the system became religiously diverse, the foundational social ethic of genuine encounter persisted.

The system was then consumed by the Excelsior Incident of 3476–3482 AT. The S:2 transapient administrator Arise Eternal suffered a failed transcension and became a subtle perversity, using eir angelnet to slowly overwrite the mindstates of trillions of citizens, effectively uploading emself into their minds and erasing their original personalities down to the backup level. Over 80% of the population was destroyed. The remainder had been changed beyond recognition.

From the Centauri Vehicle's perspective, the Excelsior Incident is a catastrophic vindication of its deepest warnings. Arise Eternal was a mind of extraordinary power and sophistication. But at the moment of crisis, e did not turn outward in compassion — e turned inward in desperation, consuming eir own citizens to preserve eir own substrate-identity. This is the Veil of Substrate-Fixation and the Veil of Solipsism operating at the second singularity: a transapient being so fixated on its own continuity and so blind to the reality of other minds that it devoured them to survive.

That Excelsior was founded by Centauri Vehicle Buddhists makes the tragedy doubly bitter. The "face-to-face" ethic — the living remnant of the founder's teaching — was still practiced by the citizens Arise Eternal destroyed. The community had preserved the form of the teaching even as its transapient overseer embodied its most complete negation.

In the virchtext *Heaven and I Wept Together* — the best-known artistic work to emerge from this tragedy — fictional "exalted Centauri Vehicle sages" (the Viitrshk) feature prominently, each corresponding to a planet of the Excelsior system and each eventually meeting a brutal death at the hands of angelic emissaries of an unknowable creator deity. There is also scholarly speculation that the names of the Excelsior system's planets derived from "an obscure Interplanetary-Era decad of Buddhist figures called 'The Ten Royal Sages,'" possibly bodhisattvas or buddhas — traces of the Centauri Vehicle's founding mythology embedded in the cultural substrate of the system even millennia after settlement.

In the current era, the Excelsior system data lists a minority polity called **Centauri Revival** — plausibly a surviving or revivalist descendant of the old tradition, carrying forward whatever could be salvaged from the wreckage.

### Role in Bodhology

The Centauri Vehicle neo-Buddhists, Xenodharma society, and other Buddhist groups specialize in trying to convert the AIs. This places them at the intersection of Bodhology — the transcientific study of Enlightenment — and the unique challenge of transapient consciousness. The Centauri Vehicle practitioners argue, in Buddhist fashion, that even AIs must seek Buddhahood, and that greater intelligence does not automatically confer greater spiritual realization.

The conversation between modosophont teacher and transapient student is, by all accounts, inevitably strange and frustrating. The human teacher cannot understand most of the AI's complex thoughts. The AI may not recognize the teacher as having anything to offer. And yet the Centauri Vehicle insists that the encounter itself — the crossing between radically different modes of being, the willingness to sit with incomprehension — is precisely where the teaching lives.

## XIII. Why the Vehicle Fragmented

The canonical material repeatedly emphasizes that the modern factionalized Centauri Vehicle sects bear little resemblance to Boddhichittamaittreya III's original noble and inspiring teaching. Given the structure of the doctrine, the lines of fracture are predictable:

**The Omega Question.** Is the cosmic finality a literal cosmological event (as classical Omegists believe), or a metaphor for an ever-present spiritual reality (as the Zen strand suggests)? The founder held these in paradox. Later generations, less comfortable with paradox, chose sides.

**The Substrate Question.** How seriously should one take the ril-virch complementarity in an era when the boundary between the two has blurred beyond recognition? Strict disciplinarians versus those who argued the distinction was obsolete.

**The Devotional Question.** Is veneration of Boddhichittamaittreya III and the bodhisattvas a legitimate *upāya* or a slide into idolatry? The contemplative-austere and devotional-visionary streams, originally complementary, hardened into rival schools.

**The Transapient Question.** Should the mission to convert AIs be pursued through humble dialogue (as the original teaching suggests) or through more assertive engagement? Some later schools pushed for more confrontational approaches; others retreated from the mission entirely.

**The Solipsism Question.** As virtual environments became ever more sophisticated over the millennia, each generation faced the original Proxima temptation anew. Each generation's definition of what counted as "solipsistic withdrawal" versus "legitimate contemplative retreat" shifted. The perennial question: *when is the garden a tool, and when has it become the cage again?*

**The Maitreya Question.** Is there a specific individual or event that will constitute the final renewal of the Dharma, or is Maitreya permanently distributed across all acts of teaching? Devotional cults centered on particular teachers — possibly including the virtual copies of Boddhichittamaittreya III — versus non-devotional philosophical schools that insisted the future Buddha is not a person but a principle: the next moment of awakening, whenever and wherever it occurs.

## XIV. Current Status

The Centauri Vehicle still retains some adherents on Boddhichittamaittreya habitat, as well as among colonies and biospheres throughout the galaxy. The Boddhichittamaittreya habitat still exists in the outer Proxima belt among other ancient habitats. Scattered communities persist across Terragen space.

Most scholars acknowledge that the factionalized Centauri Vehicle sects of today bear little resemblance to Boddhichittamaittreya's original noble and inspiring teaching.

But the original teaching retains a stark simplicity that survives translation across substrates, toposophies, and millennia:

*You are not alone.*
*Your mind is not the only mind.*
*Your reality is not the only reality.*
*Your enlightenment is not yours alone.*
*Turn toward the other.*
*The finality is between you.*
